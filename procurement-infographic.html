Procurement Strategy Document
Step 1 of 6

Purpose

Define the overall approach, objectives, and scope of the procurement exercise. Aligns stakeholders on timeline, budget, and sourcing strategy before any tender activity begins.

Key Deliverables

  • Sourcing strategy and market analysis
  • Stakeholder approval and sign-off
  • Risk assessment and mitigation plan
  • Budget envelope confirmed
  • Draft project timeline
🔍
Bid Review Program & EOI/PQQ Preparation
Step 2 of 6 · Focus Area

Purpose

Qualify the supplier market through Expressions of Interest (EOI) and Pre-Qualification Questionnaires (PQQ). The Bid Review Program ensures evaluation consistency across submissions.

Key Activities

  • Issue EOI/PQQ to market
  • Evaluate supplier capability
  • Shortlist qualified bidders
  • Draft bid review criteria
  • Conduct supplier briefings
🎯 Focus Area — Deep evaluation rigor required
⚖️
Tender RFP Issuance
Step 3 of 6

Purpose

Issue the formal Request for Proposal (RFP) to shortlisted suppliers. This document sets out all technical, commercial, and legal requirements for the engagement.

Key Deliverables

  • Complete RFP document package
  • Technical specifications issued
  • Commercial terms defined
  • Submission deadline communicated
  • NDA / confidentiality agreements
👥
Tender Query / Clarification & Mid-Tender Meetings
Step 4 of 6 · Focus Area

Purpose

Manage bidder questions, issue clarifications, and host structured mid-tender meetings to ensure all suppliers have equal access to information and can submit compliant bids.

Key Activities

  • Q&A log management
  • Addenda and clarification notices
  • Bidder presentations / site visits
  • Conflict-of-interest screening
  • Bid submission support
🎯 Focus Area — Fairness and transparency critical
🤝
Tender Evaluation and Negotiations
Step 5 of 6

Purpose

Objectively score and rank all tender submissions against pre-agreed criteria. Conduct commercial and technical negotiations with preferred suppliers to optimize value.

Key Deliverables

  • Scored evaluation report
  • Preferred supplier recommendation
  • Negotiation strategy and outcomes
  • Best and final offer (BAFO)
  • Stakeholder approval for award
📝
Contract Award and Execution
Step 6 of 6

Purpose

Formally award the contract to the successful supplier and transition into the execution phase. Notify unsuccessful bidders and initiate supplier onboarding and governance.

Key Deliverables

  • Signed contract executed
  • Award notifications issued
  • Debrief for unsuccessful bidders
  • Supplier onboarding initiated
  • Contract management plan active
